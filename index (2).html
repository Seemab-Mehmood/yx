Quantitative Youth Engagement Instrument

Measuring how far youth voice actually travels.

YX is a working instrument for the Youth Engagement Index (YEI) — the first standardized, algebraic measure of meaningful youth participation in global health governance, scored across four interdependent stages: Initiation, Inquisition, Invention, and Innovation.

Conceived and developed by Seemab Mehmood — Fatima Jinnah Medical University, Lahore · Young Working Group, World Federation of Public Health Associations — with Muhammad Asaduzzaman, University of Oslo.

The Four I's

A pathway, not a checklist.

Meaningful engagement compounds in sequence — access without inquiry stalls, inquiry without invention stalls, and invention without systemic integration is just a pilot project that never scales.

01 — ACCESS

Initiation

Institutional readiness: funding, entry portals, ring-fenced resources, and policy that lowers the threshold for youth to enter the room.

→
02 — UNDERSTAND

Inquisition

Mentorship, data access, and learning environments that let youth move from supporter to intellectual peer.

→
03 — BUILD

Invention

Safe space, research capacity, and seed funding for youth to prototype real policy, studies, and interventions.

→
04 — EMBED

Innovation

Systemic willingness to cede power — scaling youth-built solutions into permanent governance, not a pilot footnote.

Methodology

The mathematics behind the reading.

Every variable is min-max normalized 0–100 and winsorized at the 1st/99th percentile before scoring, sourced from World Bank, UNESCO, and WHO indicators alongside institutional surveys.

COMPOSITE YEI

YEI = (YI + YQ + YN + YO) / 4

Equal-weighted mean of the four pillars, each re-normalized to a 0–10 scale. <4.0 Emerging · 4.0–7.0 Developing · >7.0 Sustainable Engagement.

TIME COEFFICIENT OF YOUTH ENGAGEMENT

TC(YE) = (Σ YEIᵢ·Δtᵢ / T_total) · e^(λσ)

Adjusts the cross-sectional score for how long engagement actually lasts, penalizing tokenism via the institutional decay constant (λ) and bureaucratic friction (σ).

RESOURCE–BARRIER LOGIC

score = enabling factors / limiting barriers

Every pillar is a ratio, never a sum — so an institution can't mask structural barriers simply by spending more money.

NEGATIVE INDICATORS

e.g. Youth Inaccessibility = 100 − internet penetration

Barrier proxies are framed as what must be overcome, not what already exists — keeping the index diagnostic rather than celebratory.
